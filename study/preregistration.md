# Pre-registered analysis plan — Adaptive MOCHA

**Working title:** Adaptive MOCHA: Co-Adaptive Concept Teaching with Cognitive-Load-Aware Pacing

**Status:** Draft v1.0. To be posted to the Open Science Framework (OSF) at
`https://osf.io/<id>` **before** any main-study data is unblinded.

**Version of system used:** `adaptive-mocha v0.1.0-rc1` (see `VERSION` and
`CHANGELOG.md`). Hyper-parameter values frozen at the timestamps in
`requirements-lock.txt`.

## 1. Hypotheses

- **H1:** Post-block classifier accuracy on the held-out probe set follows
  Adaptive $\geq$ Fixed $>$ Random.
- **H2:** Raw NASA-TLX mean is lower in Adaptive than in Fixed at equivalent
  accuracy.
- **H3:** Concept-understanding quiz score follows Adaptive $\geq$ Fixed $>$
  Random.

H1 is one-tailed; H2 and H3 are one-tailed in the direction stated. All
secondary contrasts are two-tailed and Holm-Bonferroni corrected.

## 2. Design

Within-subjects 3 $\times$ 1 (Condition: Random, MOCHA-Fixed, MOCHA-Adaptive).
Counterbalancing: full 6-order Latin square; $N = 24$ assigns each order to
exactly 4 participants. Domain (D1: mixed-sentiment reviews; D2: argument
quality) is between-subjects with 12 participants per domain. A priori
power: detecting $d_z \approx 0.6$ within-subject at $\alpha = 0.05$ with
$N = 24$ yields power $\approx 0.85$.

Decision rule: if the registered analysis on $N = 24$ yields $0.05 < p <
0.10$ on H1 with the predicted direction, recruit to $N = 30$ and re-run.

## 3. Materials and conditions

- **Random:** examples drawn uniformly from the seed pool; no grouping;
  fixed pacing (`batch_size = 2`, `inter_trial_ms = 800`).
- **MOCHA-Fixed:** Variation Theory counterfactuals + Structural Alignment
  pair grouping; fixed pacing (`batch_size = 2`, `inter_trial_ms = 800`).
- **MOCHA-Adaptive:** same generator + grouper, plus rule-based controller
  with thresholds `high_load = 0.65`, `low_load = 0.35`, and behaviours
  documented in `backend/adaptive_controller.py`.

## 4. Outcomes

| Outcome | Source | Type |
|---|---|---|
| `accuracy` | `classifier_eval.accuracy` (when='post-block') | Primary |
| `macro_f1` | `classifier_eval.macro_f1` (when='post-block') | Secondary |
| `mean_raw` | `tlx.mean_raw` | Primary |
| `quiz_score` | sum of `quiz.correct` per session | Primary |
| `median_dwell_ms` | median of `labels.dwell_ms` per session | Secondary |
| qualitative themes | exit interviews | Exploratory |

## 5. Statistical analysis

Per outcome:

```
outcome ~ C(condition, Treatment(reference='random')) + (1 | participant_id)
```

fit with `statsmodels.formula.api.mixedlm`, REML, lbfgs. Pairwise contrasts
(Fixed--Random, Adaptive--Fixed, Adaptive--Random) reported with paired
$t$-tests on participant-level means, Holm-Bonferroni adjusted, Cohen's
$d_z$ included. Code: `analysis/analyze.py`.

## 6. Exclusion rules

- Sessions completed in $< 60$\% of the allotted 20-minute block are
  excluded from the within-participant analysis for that condition.
- Sessions with a system error mid-block are excluded; participants are
  invited to redo that block at a later session if possible.
- Participants who withdraw mid-session keep full compensation and are
  excluded from the analysis.

## 7. Researcher degrees of freedom

The following are locked at pre-registration:

- Seed pools, probe sets, and quiz items (versioned under `data/`).
- LLM provider and model name (`OPENAI_MODEL`, recorded in `.env`).
- Controller thresholds and rule.
- Mixed-effects model formula and contrast list.
- Figure scripts: `analysis/figures.py`.

The following remain exploratory and will be reported as such:

- Domain interaction terms (D1 vs D2) on each outcome.
- Order effects: position in the counterbalanced sequence.
- Skill-mediated effects: pre-task ML-experience covariate.

## 8. Synthetic-data audit trail

A synthetic dataset generated by `scripts/generate_synthetic_study.py --tag
mainstudy` was used to verify the analysis pipeline before any human data
was collected. That dataset is tagged `synthetic=true` in
`runs/manifest_mainstudy.csv`. It is **not** included in the analysis
reported in the paper. After IRB approval and consent, real participants
generate new SQLite files under a different tag (`tag=chi27`), and the
final report is built only from those.
