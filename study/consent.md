## What is this study about?

We are studying how people teach computers to recognize concepts (for example, whether a movie review is positive or negative, or whether an argument is well supported). You will use three different teaching interfaces and answer a few short questionnaires.

## What will I do?

- Read short texts (about 1-3 sentences each) and label them.
- Answer brief workload questions between blocks.
- Take a short quiz after each block.
- Take part in a 5-minute interview at the end (audio recorded if you agree).

Total time: about 75 minutes.

## Are there any risks?

The task is similar to everyday reading. Some texts may contain mild opinion or argument content. You may stop at any time without penalty.

## Will my data be kept private?

- We do not collect your name, email, or any identifying information in the study logs.
- Audio interviews are transcribed and the recordings deleted within 30 days.
- Anonymized data may be shared in academic publications and public archives (such as the Open Science Framework). You cannot be identified from the shared data.

## Compensation

You will receive **USD 20** (or local equivalent) for completing the session. You receive full compensation even if you stop early after starting.

## Voluntary participation

Your participation is voluntary. You may decline any question, skip any task, or withdraw at any time without giving a reason and without penalty.
