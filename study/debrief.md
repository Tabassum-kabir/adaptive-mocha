# Debrief — Adaptive MOCHA

Thank you for taking part. Here is what the study was actually about.

You used three different versions of a "machine teaching" interface:

1. **Random** — examples were drawn at random.
2. **MOCHA-Fixed** — examples were chosen and grouped using two cognitive-science theories (Variation Theory and Structural Alignment Theory), with a fixed pace.
3. **MOCHA-Adaptive** — same as Fixed, but the system tried to adapt its pace and difficulty to how easy or hard the task seemed for you, based on how long you took and how you answered some small calibration questions.

We are comparing these three conditions to see whether adapting to a person's cognitive load improves both how well the system learns and how the task feels to you.

You may have noticed that some examples seemed easier or harder than others. That was intentional in two of the three conditions. The "right" labels for some borderline examples are genuinely debatable — this is a feature of the study, not a sign you got something wrong.

The classifier you taught is built on a large language model running through an API. None of your free-text responses were sent to that API. Only the system-curated examples and your label choices were used.
